YayOrNay - Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 26, 2026

Overview

YayOrNay is a social polling app that lets you ask friends yes/no or this-or-that questions and vote on theirs. Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and what choices you have.

Data controller: Cem Karabulut. Contact: cmkarabulut@gmail.com.

Data We Collect

Account information. When you sign in with Apple, Google, or email/password, we receive your email address and (with Apple or Google) your display name. You choose a username and, optionally, a profile photo during onboarding. These are stored in Firebase Authentication and Cloud Firestore.

Questions, votes, and comments. Content you create — yes/no questions, this-or-that questions, attached photos, votes, comments, and @-mentions — is stored in Cloud Firestore and Cloud Storage. Private questions are visible only to the recipients you send them to. Public questions are visible to any signed-in YayOrNay user.

Friend connections. Your friend list, pending friend requests, blocked users, and friend groups are stored so you can organize who you interact with.

Push notification token. If you enable notifications, we store a device token issued by Apple Push Notification service and Firebase Cloud Messaging so we can deliver alerts. You can toggle individual notification categories (new question, new vote, new comment, mentions, friend requests, friend accepted) in Settings.

Vote timestamps. When you vote on a question, we record a timestamp alongside your vote. This lets us produce aggregated usage analytics (e.g. how many votes happen per day).

Analytics events. We log app interaction events — signups, posts, votes, skips, comments, friend actions, tab switches, and similar — along with your account ID. We use these events to measure feature adoption, identify bugs, and prioritize improvements. Events do not contain the text of your questions, your photos, or the contents of your posts — only the category of action and a small set of structured parameters (for example, the kind of question, whether it was public or to friends, and whether it had a photo). The data is processed via Firebase Analytics (linked to Google Analytics 4) and exported to BigQuery for our own analytical queries.

Moderation reports. If you report a question as inappropriate, we record which user reported which question so moderation doesn't double-count and the reported content can be reviewed.

We do not collect your location, contacts, health data, financial data, browsing history outside the app, or identifiers used for advertising.

How We Use Your Data

We use your data to:

  • Authenticate your account.
  • Deliver core features: posting, voting, commenting, notifications, friend connections, the public feed, and moderation.
  • Send push notifications you've opted into, respecting per-category toggles.
  • Understand how the app is used. Analytics events are tagged with your account ID server-side so we can answer questions like "do users who add friends post more?" or "where do new users drop off in onboarding?" — but we don't share this data externally and don't use it for advertising or profiling.

We do not use your data for advertising, cross-app tracking, or profiling.

Third-Party Services

We share data with the infrastructure providers we depend on to operate the app:

  • Google LLC (Firebase + Google Cloud) processes authentication (Firebase Auth), database storage (Cloud Firestore), file storage (Cloud Storage), server logic (Cloud Functions), push notification routing (Firebase Cloud Messaging), abuse prevention (Firebase App Check), image safety scanning (Cloud Vision SafeSearch — every uploaded photo is scanned for adult, violent, or racy content before it is shown to anyone else), and product analytics (Firebase Analytics linked to Google Analytics 4, with a daily export of raw events to BigQuery for our own analytical queries). See Google's privacy policy and Cloud Vision data usage.
  • Apple Inc. processes Sign in with Apple and delivers push notifications via Apple Push Notification service. See Apple's privacy policy.
  • OpenAI, L.L.C. processes the text of every question and comment for content safety classification (hate, harassment, sexual content, violence, self-harm). OpenAI also processes the text and any attached photos of questions to categorize each post into broad topic tags (e.g. fashion, food, technology) that personalize how questions are ranked in your feed. Content is sent for the sole purpose of scoring or categorization; OpenAI does not retain it for training. See OpenAI's API data usage policy.

Other YayOrNay users see the public parts of your profile (username, display name, optional photo) and whatever content you share with them. We do not sell your data or share it with advertisers or any third party not listed above.

Where Your Data Is Stored

Data is stored on Google Cloud infrastructure in regions Google assigns to your Firebase project (typically the United States or European Union). Google operates standard contractual clauses and a Data Processing Addendum for international transfers.

How Long We Keep Your Data

  • While your account is active, we retain your profile and content so the app continues to work.
  • When you delete your account (Settings → Delete account), we remove your profile, questions, comments, votes, friend connections, photos, notification records, and authentication account within seven days.
  • Aggregated, anonymized analytics may be retained after deletion since they no longer identify you.

Your Rights

You can:

  • Access the data we hold about you (email us for a copy).
  • Correct your profile in-app (Profile → Edit).
  • Delete your account and associated data in-app (Settings → Delete account).
  • Export your data (email us for a portable copy).
  • Object to specific processing or withdraw consent (email us).

If you live in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority. Our lawful basis for processing is consent (for optional features like notifications) and performance of contract (for core app features you signed up for).

If you live in California, the CCPA grants you the right to know what we collect, to delete it, to correct it, and to non-discrimination for exercising your rights. We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, so no opt-out mechanism is required.

Moderation and Safety

YayOrNay applies multiple layers of moderation to user content:

  • Pre-publication image scanning. Every uploaded photo is automatically scanned by Google Cloud Vision SafeSearch. Photos flagged as adult, violent, or racy are removed and the associated post is hidden.
  • Pre-publication text scanning. Every question and comment is automatically scanned by OpenAI's content moderation classifier. Text flagged for hate, harassment, sexual content, violence, or self-harm is hidden.
  • In-app reporting. You can report any question or comment from inside the app. Reports on public content trigger immediate auto-hide pending human review. Reports on private content auto-hide after a small number of independent reports.
  • Blocking. You can block any user from any surface where they appear (profile, comment, mention, voter list). Blocking is one-sided, persistent, and removes the friendship if one existed.

We aim to review every report within 24 hours of submission. Confirmed violations result in content removal and, for repeated or severe violations, account suspension or permanent ban. Full rules and enforcement details are in our Terms of Use.

Security

  • All data in transit uses TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Passwords are hashed and managed by Firebase Authentication; we never see your plaintext password.
  • Backend access is protected by Firebase App Check (App Attest on iOS) and Firestore security rules that restrict reads and writes to the authenticated user.
  • We do not access your photo library beyond what you explicitly select via the system picker.

Children's Privacy

YayOrNay is not intended for children under 13, or under 16 in the European Economic Area. We do not knowingly collect data from children under those ages. If you believe a child has created an account, email us and we will delete it.
